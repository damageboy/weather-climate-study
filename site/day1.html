Day 1

Weather vs Climate & Measuring the Weather

Learn how scientists measure the weather — and how weather differs from climate.

Progress: 0%

📖 Learn

Weather

Weather is the state of the atmosphere at a given time. It can change from hour to hour. Examples: rainy, sunny, windy, foggy.

Climate

Climate is the average weather in a place over a long period (usually 30 years). It changes much more slowly than weather.

🌡 Measuring Instruments
🌡️

Thermometer

Measures temperature. Kept in a Stevenson screen (shaded white box). Sited: 1.2 m above ground, away from buildings.

°C (degrees Celsius)
💨

Anemometer

Measures wind speed. Cups spin in the wind. Sited: high up in the open, away from trees and buildings.

mph or km/h
🔵

Barometer

Measures air pressure. If pressure falls → rain coming. If pressure rises → fair weather. Sited: indoors or in a screen.

millibars (mb)
🐓

Wind Vane

Measures wind direction. Points into the wind. "SW wind" means wind from the south-west. Sited: high, open location.

Compass bearing (N, NE, SW…)
🌧️

Rain Gauge

Measures precipitation (rain, hail, snow). Sited: outdoors, flush with ground level, away from trees.

millimetres (mm)
👁️

Visibility Meter

Measures visibility — how far you can see. A beam of light is sent out and a sensor measures how much arrives. Sited: open area.

metres or kilometres
