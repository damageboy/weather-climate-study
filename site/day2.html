Clouds & The Three Types of Rainfall

Learn how clouds form and why rain falls in three different ways.

Progress: 0%

📖 Learn
☁️ Cloud Types
⛅

Cumulus

Fluffy white clouds, low in the sky. Can grow tall and dark. Bring short heavy showers.

🌫️

Stratus

Big blankets of dull grey cloud hanging low. Cover the whole sky. Give a light drizzle but no real showers.

🌀

Cirrus

Thin wispy clouds very high up (over 6 km). So cold they are made of ice crystals. Mean bad weather is coming.

🌧️ Three Types of Rainfall

☀️ Convectional Rainfall

  • The sun heats the ground, which warms the air above it.
  • Warm air rises in convection currents.
  • Rising air cools. Water vapour condenses to form clouds.
  • Clouds grow and it rains.

Common in summer, inland areas, far from the sea — where the ground gets hottest.

⛰️ Relief Rainfall

  • Moist air (often from the ocean) blows towards a mountain or hill.
  • The air is forced to rise over the mountain.
  • As it rises, it cools. Water vapour condenses → clouds form → it rains on the windward side.
  • The air descends on the other side (leeward / rain shadow) and warms — so it stays dry.

In the UK the prevailing wind is from the south-west, so the west coast gets lots of relief rainfall.

🌬️ Frontal Rainfall

  • A warm air mass meets a cold air mass.
  • The warm air is lighter, so it slides up and over the cold air.
  • As the warm air rises, it cools. Water vapour condenses → clouds form → it rains.
  • Frontal rain can fall almost anywhere. In the UK it's common from Atlantic air masses.
