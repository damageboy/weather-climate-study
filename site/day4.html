Factors Influencing Climate & Climate Graphs

Why is Egypt hotter than the UK? Why is the west coast wetter? Find out today!

Progress: 0%

📖 Learn — Factors That Affect Climate
🌍

Latitude

The main factor. Further from the equator → cooler. The Earth is curved, so the sun's rays spread over a larger area at higher latitudes. That's why the UK is cooler than Egypt.

⛰️

Altitude

Higher above sea level → colder. Temperature drops by about 1°C for every 100 m you climb. Ben Nevis (1344 m) has snow in summer!

🌊

Distance from the Sea

The sea warms and cools slowly. Coastal areas: cooler in summer, warmer in winter than inland. A sea breeze cools the coast in summer.

💨

Prevailing Wind

The UK's prevailing wind blows from the south-west. It picks up moisture from the Atlantic → brings rain, especially to the west coast.

🌀

Ocean Currents

The North Atlantic Drift (a warm ocean current) flows past the UK's west coast. It warms the wind, keeping winters milder than they would otherwise be.

🏙️

Other Local Factors

Shelter (hills blocking wind), urban heat islands (cities are warmer due to heat from buildings and traffic).

📊 Climate Graphs

How to read a climate graph

A climate graph shows average monthly temperature (as a red/orange line) and average monthly rainfall (as blue bars) for a place across 12 months.

Temperature is read on the right y-axis (°C). Rainfall is read on the left y-axis (mm).
