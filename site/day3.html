Day 3

Air Pressure & Weather

Discover how air pressure shapes the weather — from sunny days to stormy nights.

Progress: 0%

📖 Learn

🔵 Low Pressure

  • Warm air rises at the centre.
  • Rising air cools → clouds form.
  • Clouds bring rain and wind.
  • A falling barometer signals low pressure coming.

Low pressure = unsettled, stormy, windy, rainy weather.

🔴 High Pressure

  • Cold air sinks at the centre.
  • Sinking air warms → no condensation.
  • No clouds form → clear, dry skies.
  • A rising barometer signals high pressure coming.

High pressure = settled, dry, calm weather.

☀️ High Pressure in Summer
☀️

Hot sunny days

No clouds to block the sun.

💧

Dew at night

No cloud blanket → ground cools fast → water vapour condenses on grass.

🌵

Drought

Long periods of no rain can cause drought.

⛈️

Thunderstorms

Very hot inland days → air rises rapidly → huge black clouds → thunderstorms and flooding.

❄️ High Pressure in Winter
🌨️

Frost

No cloud blanket → ground cools rapidly at night → water vapour freezes on surfaces.

🌫️

Fog

Water vapour condenses on dust and tiny particles in the air → thick fog, dangerous driving.

🧊

Ice

Water on roads freezes as the sun goes down.

😊

Clear cold days

No cloud → clear blue skies but very cold temperatures.
